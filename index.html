AIRHILT: A Human-in-the-Loop Testbed for Multimodal Conflict Detection in Aviation

Under double-blind review
Download Paper (PDF) Supplementary (code/data)

Abstract

We introduce AIRHILT (Aviation Integrated Reasoning, Human-in-the-Loop Testbed), a modular simulation environment for evaluating multimodal pilot/ATC assistance systems for conflict detection. Built on Godot, AIRHILT synchronizes radio communications (ASR/TTS), camera-based visual perception, and ADS-B surveillance in a unified, reproducible platform with human-in-the-loop scenarios across terminal-area and en-route operations. Standardized JSON/REST interfaces let researchers swap ASR, vision, decision, and TTS modules. We show a reference pipeline (fine-tuned Whisper, YOLO-based detection, ADS-B logic, and structured LLM reasoning) with preliminary results on representative conflict scenarios. Artifacts here are provided for anonymous peer review.
